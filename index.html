Joseph Stiglitz

An Economist First!

  • Recieved the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Science in 2001 "for laying the foundations for the theory of markets with asymmetric information".(1)
  • Former vice president and chief economist of the World Bank.
  • Appointed as chairman of the U.N. Commission on Reforms of the International Monetary and Financial System in 2009.
  • Appointed chair of the international Commission on the Measurement of Economic Performance and Social Progress in 2010.
  • He has received more than 40 honorary degrees, including from Harvard, Oxford, and Cambridge Universities and been decorated by several governments
  • Based on academic citations, Stiglitz is the 4th most influential economist in the world today. (2)
  • Authored and co-authored 31 books amongst dozens of white papers; on modern inequality, growth developement, and social progress.
  • Adviser to president Obama, though critical of the financial-industry rescue plan; saying whoever designed the plan is "in the pocket of the banks". (3)
  • In 2011, he was named by Time magazine as one of the 100 most influential people in the world.
